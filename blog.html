Three Days on Grand Manan: Puffins, Whales & Sea Fog

A 7 a.m. rental pickup, a drive to Blacks Harbour, and a ferry I almost missed. I arrived about thirty minutes before departure, boarded from the non-reserved lane, and ninety minutes later Grand Manan appeared through the morning haze. Three days on the island turned into one of my favourite photography trips so far.

Day 1 — Coastal Landmarks

Swallowtail Lighthouse was my first stop. From the lookout I used the shoreline as a leading line to frame the returning ferry before following the cliffs toward a group of seals swimming below. The hike to the Hole in the Wall became longer than expected after I missed the return trail, but the basalt arch was worth every extra step. The day finished with sea combing and a quiet sunset before checking into the cottage.

Day 2 — Wildlife

The highlight of the trip was Machias Seal Island. From inside a small wooden blind, Atlantic Puffins and Razorbills wandered to within only a few feet. Nearly everything was photographed at 200 mm using continuous autofocus and burst shooting to keep up with their constant movement. On the way back we stopped to watch seals before heading straight into a whale-watching tour where several humpbacks surfaced nearby. The evening ended overlooking the towering volcanic cliffs at Southwest Head.

Day 3 — Sea Fog

The final morning arrived under thick fog. High tide ruled out testing the island's magnetic sand, but the shoreline still offered coral, snail eggs, and one last surprise—a crane catching and eating a crab. Before catching the ferry home from Dark Harbour I photographed the bird at the long end of the lens, isolating it against the muted shoreline.

Throughout the trip I worked across the Tamron's full zoom range, switching between wide landscapes and tight wildlife compositions. Everything was captured in RAW, leaving plenty of flexibility for editing before these images make their way into the gallery.
